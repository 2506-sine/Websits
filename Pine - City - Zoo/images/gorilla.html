GORILLA

The gorilla is the largest living primate and is known for its impressive size, strength, and intelligence. Gorillas are primarily herbivorous, feeding on leaves, stems, fruit, and flowers. They are social animals that live in groups called troops, which are typically found in dense forests in central Africa.

Gorillas are known for their strong family bonds and social structure. A troop is usually led by a dominant males. Gorillas are also known for their impressive vocalizations, which include grunts, roars, and hoots. They communicate with each other using a variety of vocalizations and body language.

Gorillas are primarily herbivorous, feeding on leaves, stems, fruit, and flowers. They are also known for their impressive strength and agility, which they use to navigate their forest habitats and evade predators.

Gorillas are found in a variety of habitats, including tropical and subtropical forests, montane forests, and bamboo forests. They are native to central Africa, and their populations are threatened by habitat loss and poaching.

In summary, the gorilla is a large, intelligent primate that is well-adapted to its forest habitat. With its impressive size, strength, and social behavior, it is a fascinating species that plays an important role in its ecosystem.

Gorillas are the largest living primates, reaching heights between 1.25 and 1.8 m (4 ft 1 in and 5 ft 11 in), weights between 100 and 270 kg (220 and 600 lb), and arm spans up to 2.6 m (8 ft 6 in), depending on species and sex. They tend to live in troops, with the leader being called a silverback. The eastern gorilla is distinguished from the western by darker fur colour and some other minor morphological differences. Gorillas tend to live 35-40 years in the wild.
